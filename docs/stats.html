Douaa Mugahid Harvard School of Public Health Professor (or other Faculty) I fully endorse this letter, and will add that there is a need for the federal government to investigate what a reasonable APC is for federally-funded research. Not doing a proper analysis on this was one of the shortcomings of plan S. Otherwise, as the letter says, APCs will be set at ridiculous, even more exclusionary prices. There also needs to be a re-evaluation of how federal agencies such as the NIH and NSF value publishing in the most expensive journals when evaluating grant recipients based on their previous publication record. The latter calls for a major shift in the current incentive system (and academic culture) that puts a lot of weight on the venue of a publication as opposed to the value and contributions of the work.
